PARTICIPANT INFORMATION SHEET

How do images impact consumer psychology and influence the prices buyers are prepared to offer?

We are conducting a research study to understand how images impact consumer psychology and influence the prices buyers are prepared to offer for real estate. This survey aims to gather data that will help us delve into the intricacies of buyer preferences and decision-making processes in the property market.

Our research is grounded in the field of behavioural finance and housing economics. It focuses on the psychological factors that play a significant role in property valuation. Existing literature, including studies by Whittle et al., Lux Martin, and Mohammad, Ali & Jasimin, suggests that human decisions in the housing market are not always based on rational calculations. Instead, they are often swayed by cognitive biases, societal norms, and emotional influences.

The hypothesis driving this study is that different groups of buyers will demonstrate distinct preferences for specific types of real estate images. These preferences are thought to be influenced by factors such as lifestyle, life stage, and investment objectives. We anticipate that these preferences will be reflected in how buyers perceive the value of properties and in their willingness to engage or offer higher prices.

Your participation in this survey will involve viewing a series of real estate images and providing your perceptions and estimated valuations for these properties. Your contributions are invaluable to our study and will help in advancing our understanding of consumer behaviour in the real estate market. All responses will be kept confidential and will be used solely for the purpose of this research.

You may withdraw from the survey at any time during the questionnaire. All information collected as part of this study will be retained for seven years in a secured computer server in the School of Business.
